Case Studies

Case Study 01

Scaling E-Commerce Marketplace Operations

55%

The Problem

Jumia Kenya's seller base was stagnant. Seller drop-off during onboarding was high, category depth was thin, and the marketplace lacked the density needed to compete on assortment and price.

Strategy & Execution

Designed and executed a targeted seller acquisition programme segmented by product category. Built a structured incubation model — pairing new vendors with dedicated account support, performance benchmarks, and milestone-based escalation — to compress time-to-first-sale and reduce early churn.

Measurable Outcome

Achieved 55% marketplace growth in active vendor count and Gross Merchandise Volume. Category depth expanded significantly, improving customer conversion rates and repeat purchase frequency across targeted segments.

Case Study 02

Operational Efficiency & Data-Driven Decision Making

93%NPS

The Problem

Fragmented communication channels between vendors, operations, and finance were creating payment bottlenecks, rising fraud cases, and significant operational blindspots. Leadership lacked a unified view of performance health.

Strategy & Execution

Deployed a Power BI analytics suite — consolidating vendor, finance, and operations data into a real-time executive dashboard. Streamlined cross-functional communication protocols and introduced structured fraud detection workflows with clear escalation paths and SLA accountability.

Measurable Outcome

Fraud cases dropped dramatically. Payment resolution cycles shortened from weeks to days. Vendor satisfaction soared, culminating in a sustained 92–95% Vendor Net Promoter Score — a benchmark for the Jumia group.

Case Study 03

Product Quality Safeguarding & Return Mitigation

↓ Returns

The Problem

Defective and counterfeit items entering the fulfilment network were inflating return rates, eroding customer trust, and generating significant direct costs — including expensive failed return pick-up attempts.

Strategy & Execution

Conceptualised and launched the 'Product Quality Check' project — a pre-dispatch inspection programme that flagged defective and non-genuine items before they reached customers. Simultaneously redesigned return pick-up SLAs, reducing failed collection attempts through route optimisation and proactive vendor communication.

Measurable Outcome

Return costs reduced substantially for the business. Customer complaint rates tied to product quality declined. The initiative was adopted as a standard operating procedure and contributed directly to improved post-purchase satisfaction scores across the platform.
